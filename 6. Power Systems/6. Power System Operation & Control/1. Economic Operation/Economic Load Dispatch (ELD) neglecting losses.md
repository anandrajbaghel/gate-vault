### Economic Load Dispatch (ELD) neglecting losses
#economic-load-dispatch #optimization #power-system-operation

> ==**Economic Load Dispatch (ELD)** is the process of allocating the total load demand among the available online generating units in such a way that the total cost of generation is minimized, while satisfying all system constraints.== The version neglecting losses is a foundational model that assumes the transmission network is lossless.

#### Objective and Constraints
#eld/objective-function #eld/constraints

The ==primary goal of ELD is to solve an optimization problem==.

1. **Objective Function: Minimize Total Fuel Cost ($C_T$)**
    The total fuel cost is the sum of the individual costs of each generating unit.
    $$ C_T = \sum_{i=1}^{N} C_i(P_{Gi}) \quad (\text{Rs/hr}) $$
    ==where $C_i(P_{Gi})$ is the cost function of the $i$-th generator and $P_{Gi}$ is its real power output.==

2. **Constraint Equation: Power Balance**
    The ==total power generated by all units must equal the total system demand ($P_D$), as transmission losses are ignored==.
    $$\boxed{\quad \sum_{i=1}^{N} P_{Gi} = P_D \quad}$$

#### Input-Output and Fuel Cost Characteristics
#fuel-cost-curve

The ==fuel cost function $C_i$ for a thermal generating unit is typically represented by a quadratic function of its real power output $P_{Gi}$==:
$$\boxed{\quad C_i(P_{Gi}) = a_i P_{Gi}^2 + b_i P_{Gi} + c_i \quad (\text{Rs/hr}) \quad}$$
where:
- ==$P_{Gi}$ is the power output in MW.==
- ==$a_i, b_i, c_i$ are the cost coefficients of the $i$-th unit.== <u>The $c_i$ term represents no-load costs and does not affect the dispatch, only the total cost calculation.</u>

> [!memory] Economic Dispatch – Always?
> ==An increase in a generator’s cost coefficient reduces its dispatch and increases total cost only if the generator remains unconstrained and the same optimality conditions apply.==

---
#### Incremental Fuel Cost (IFC) and Optimality Condition
#incremental-fuel-cost #equal-lambda-criterion

The **Incremental Fuel Cost (IFC)** is the rate of change of cost with respect to power output. It represents the cost of producing the next megawatt (MW) of power.
$$ IC_i = \frac{dC_i}{dP_{Gi}} \quad (\text{Rs/MWh}) $$
For the standard quadratic cost function, the IFC is a linear function:
$$ IC_i = 2a_i P_{Gi} + b_i $$

==The fundamental condition for the most economical division of load between generating units is that all participating units must operate at the **same incremental fuel cost**. This common value is known as the system incremental cost or **system lambda ($\lambda$)**.==
$$\boxed{\quad IC_1(P_{G1}) = IC_2(P_{G2}) = \dots = IC_N(P_{GN}) = \lambda \quad}$$

> [!hint] Intuition
> If unit $A$ has a lower incremental cost than unit $B$ ($IC_A < IC_B$), we could save money by decreasing the output of the more expensive unit $B$ and increasing the output of the cheaper unit $A$ by the same amount, resulting in a net cost reduction. This process continues until their incremental costs are equal.

#### Solution Algorithm (Without Losses)
#eld/algorithm

The ELD problem is solved analytically using the method of Lagrange multipliers. The solution follows these steps:

1.  For each generator $i$, express its power output $P_{Gi}$ in terms of the system lambda, $\lambda$, using the optimality condition:
    $2a_i P_{Gi} + b_i = \lambda \implies \boldsymbol{P_{Gi} = \frac{\lambda - b_i}{2a_i}}$

2.  Substitute these expressions for each $P_{Gi}$ into the power balance constraint:
    $$ \sum_{i=1}^{N} \frac{\lambda - b_i}{2a_i} = P_D $$

3.  Solve the resulting linear equation for the unknown system lambda, $\lambda$:
    $$\begin{align}
    \lambda \sum_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{2a_i} - \sum_{i=1}^{N} \frac{b_i}{2a_i} &= P_D \\
    \implies \boxed{\quad \lambda = \frac{P_D + \sum_{i=1}^{N} \frac{b_i}{2a_i}}{\sum_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{2a_i}} \quad}
    \end{align}$$

4.  Substitute the calculated value of $\lambda$ back into the expression for each $P_{Gi}$ to find the optimal power dispatch for each unit.

---
#### Considering Generator Operating Limits
#generator-limits #eld/constraints

In reality, every generator has minimum ($P_{Gi,min}$) and maximum ($P_{Gi,max}$) real power output limits. The optimal solution must satisfy:
$$ P_{Gi,min} \le P_{Gi} \le P_{Gi,max} $$
**Procedure if a limit is violated:**

1.  Calculate the initial dispatch using the method above.
2.  Check if any generator's output $P_{Gi}$ violates its limits.
3.  If $P_{Gi} < P_{Gi,min}$, set its output to the minimum limit: $P_{Gi} = P_{Gi,min}$.
4.  If $P_{Gi} > P_{Gi,max}$, set its output to the maximum limit: $P_{Gi} = P_{Gi,max}$.
5.  The unit whose power is fixed at a limit no longer participates in the economic dispatch. Its incremental cost will *not* be equal to the system $\lambda$.
6.  The remaining load ($P_{D,new} = P_D - P_{Gi,fixed}$) is then re-dispatched among the remaining generators by solving a new ELD problem for them (i.e., recalculate $\lambda$ for the remaining units).
7.  Repeat this process until all generator outputs are within their limits.
