### Reluctance
*Magnetic Circuits*

Opposition to the flow of [[magnetic flux]] in a material, analogous to electrical resistance in circuits.

---
1. Ampere-turns per weber ($At/Wb$)
2. Inverse henry ($1/H$)
$$\mathcal{R} = \frac{l}{\mu A}$$
- $l$ is the length of the magnetic path (in meters, $m$),
- $\mu$ is the permeability of the material (in Henries per meter, $H/m$),
- $A$ is the cross-sectional area of the magnetic path (in square meters, $m^2$)
---
1. Ampere-turns ($At$) refers to the [[magnetomotive force]] ($MMF$), which is the driving force that causes the magnetic circuit.
2. Weber ($Wb$) is the unit of [[magnetic flux]].
