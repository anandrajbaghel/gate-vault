### Power
*Electrical & Mechanical Systems*

The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred.

---
#### Formulas

- **General:** $P = \dfrac{dE}{dt}$
    
- **From Kinetic Energy:**  
    $$KE = \tfrac{1}{2} m v^2 \rightarrow \boxed{\quad P = \dfrac{d(KE)}{dt} = Fv\quad}$$
    $$KE = \frac{1}{2}J\omega^2 \rightarrow \boxed{\quad P = \frac{d(KE)}{dt} = J\omega \cdot \frac{d\omega}{dt}=J\omega \cdot \alpha \quad}$$
    
- **Rotational Systems (Machines):**
	$$P = T \omega$$
    
- **Electrical Power:**  
    $$P = VI = I^2R = \dfrac{V^2}{R}$$

*(Very important  for motors, generators, and swing equation analysis.)*
