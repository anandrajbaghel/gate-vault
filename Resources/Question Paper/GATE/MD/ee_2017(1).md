16. Consider $g(t) = \begin{cases} t - \lfloor t \rfloor, & t \ge 0 \\ t - \lceil t \rceil, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$, where $t \in \mathbb{R}$.
	Here, $\lfloor t \rfloor$ represents the largest integer less than or equal to $t$ and $\lceil t \rceil$ denotes the smallest integer greater than or equal to $t$. The coefficient of the second harmonic component of the Fourier series representing $g(t)$ is `______`. [**1 Marks**] ^q16

#signals-and-systems #fourier-series

>[!success]- Answer
>**Marks to All** is the correct answer
>
>---
>**Note**: This question was marked as "Marks to All" in the official key. The function g(t) = t - floor(t) is a standard sawtooth wave. Its Fourier series coefficient $c_n$ for $n\neq0$ is $\frac{j}{2\pi n}$. The second harmonic corresponds to $n=2$, so the coefficient is $c_2 = \frac{j}{4\pi}$. The magnitude is $\frac{1}{4\pi} \approx 0.0795$. The question's ambiguity might be the reason for its cancellation.

> [!example] Topics to Refer
> [[Fourier Series of Periodic Signals]]
