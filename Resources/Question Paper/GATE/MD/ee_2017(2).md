12. In the circuit shown, the diodes are ideal, the inductance is small, and $I_0 \neq 0$. Which one of the following statements is true? [**1 Marks**] [<span style="color:#fc3d03;font-weight:500">-1/3 Marks</span>] ![[gate-2017-ee2-q12.png]]
	(A) $D_1$ conducts for greater than 180° and $D_2$ conducts for greater than 180°.
	(B) $D_2$ conducts for more than 180° and $D_1$ conducts for 180°.
	(C) $D_1$ conducts for 180° and $D_2$ conducts for 180°.
	(D) $D_1$ conducts for more than 180° and $D_2$ conducts for 180°. ^q12

#power-electronics #rectifiers #freewheeling-diode

>[!success]- Answer
>**(A) $D_1$ conducts for greater than 180° and $D_2$ conducts for greater than 180°.** is the correct answer
>
>---
>**Note:** The circuit is a single-phase center-tapped rectifier with a constant current load, which is unusual. However, if we interpret it as a circuit where the source provides current and the diodes direct it, diode $D_1$ would conduct during the positive half-cycle and $D_2$ during the negative. The source inductance, although small, will cause overlap, making both conduction angles slightly greater than 180°. Without the inductor, they would be 180°.

> [!example] Topics to Refer
> [[Single-Phase Rectifiers]]
> [[Commutation Angle]]
