>[!success]- Answer

> ---
> **Note:** The official answer key states (C), but based on standard statistical theory, $\hat{\sigma}_2^2$ is the unbiased estimator of the variance, meaning $E(\hat{\sigma}_2^2) = \sigma_0^2$.
> $\hat{\sigma}_1^2$ is a biased estimator. There seems to be an error in the question or the provided answer. The question states mean zero, so the formula for variance simplifies, and for a known mean (0), $\hat{\sigma}_1^2$ is indeed the unbiased estimator.
